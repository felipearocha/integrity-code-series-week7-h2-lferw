1. Hydrogen Diffusion — Oriani Stress-Assisted Fick's Law

1.1 Through-wall diffusion PDE

$$ \frac{\partial C_L}{\partial t} \;=\; D_L\,\frac{\partial^2 C_L}{\partial x^2} \;+\; D_L\,\frac{V_H}{RT}\,\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\!\left(C_L\,\frac{\partial \sigma_h}{\partial x}\right) $$ with lattice concentration $C_L$ [mol/m³], lattice diffusivity $D_L$, partial molar volume of H in Fe $V_H$, and hydrostatic stress $\sigma_h$. Solved by the method of lines (central differences in space, explicit stepping with a Courant stability limit $\Delta t \le \Delta x^2/2D_L$).

SOURCE: Oriani stress-assisted Fick's law [SOURCE]. Constants $D_L=1.3\times10^{-10}$ m²/s, $V_H=2.0\times10^{-6}$ m³/mol — $D_L$ [ASSUMED] (typical BCC iron at 298 K); $V_H$ literature consensus.

Code: src/hydrogen_diffusion.py → solve_diffusion.

1.2 Boundary and initial conditions

$$ C_L(0,t) = C_0 = S\sqrt{p_{H_2}}, \qquad C_L(w,t) = 0, \qquad C_L(x,0) = 0 $$ Inner surface loaded by Sievert's law; outer surface held at zero (atmosphere / cathodic protection); pipe wall initially hydrogen-free from prior natural-gas service.

SOURCE: Sievert's law surface uptake [SOURCE]; Sievert's constant $S=0.08$ mol/(m³·MPa$^{0.5}$) [ASSUMED] — requires experimental measurement.

Code: src/hydrogen_diffusion.py → sieverts_surface_concentration.

2. Wall Stress Field — Lame Thick-Cylinder Solution

$$ \sigma_r = \frac{p\,r_i^2}{r_o^2-r_i^2}\!\left(1-\frac{r_o^2}{r^2}\right), \qquad \sigma_\theta = \frac{p\,r_i^2}{r_o^2-r_i^2}\!\left(1+\frac{r_o^2}{r^2}\right), \qquad \sigma_z = \frac{p\,r_i^2}{r_o^2-r_i^2} $$ $$ \sigma_h = \tfrac{1}{3}\left(\sigma_r + \sigma_\theta + \sigma_z\right) $$ Elastic Lame solution for a closed-end thick-walled cylinder under internal pressure; the hydrostatic component $\sigma_h$ drives the stress-assisted diffusion term in §1.1.

SOURCE: Lame thick-cylinder elasticity solution, closed-end axial condition [SOURCE].

Code: src/hydrogen_diffusion.py → compute_stress_field.

3. Pit Growth at the ERW Seam & Murakami SIF

3.1 Power-law pit growth

$$ a_{\text{pit}}(t) \;=\; k_{\text{pit}}\,f_{\text{seam}}\,t^{\,n_{\text{pit}}} $$ with $f_{\text{seam}}$ the ERW-seam selective-corrosion enhancement and $t$ in service years. Pit depth is capped at $0.8\,w$ (physical surface-pit limit).

SOURCE: power-law pit growth from prior NG service. Constants $k_{\text{pit}}=5\times10^{-5}$ m·yr$^{-n}$, $n_{\text{pit}}=0.5$, $f_{\text{seam}}=2.0$ — all [ASSUMED] (moderate corrosion with partial CP; diffusion-limited exponent).

Code: src/pit_to_crack.py → pit_depth_after_ng_service.

3.2 Murakami $\sqrt{\text{area}}$ stress intensity

$$ K_{\text{pit}} \;=\; 0.65\,\sigma\,\sqrt{\pi\sqrt{\text{area}}}, \qquad \text{area} = \frac{\pi}{2}\,c\,a, \qquad c = (c/a)\,a $$ The $0.65$ prefactor applies to surface-breaking defects (vs 0.50 internal); the projected area is that of a semi-elliptical surface pit of depth $a$ and half-length $c$.

SOURCE: Murakami $\sqrt{\text{area}}$ parameter for small surface defects [SOURCE]. Aspect ratio $c/a=2.0$ [ASSUMED].

Code: src/pit_to_crack.py → murakami_sif, pit_geometry.

3.3 Barlow hoop stress

$$ \sigma_h \;=\; \frac{p\,D}{2\,t} $$

SOURCE: Barlow's formula (thin-wall hoop stress) [SOURCE].

4. Pit-to-Crack Transition

4.1 Hydrogen-degraded fatigue threshold

$$ K_{th}(C_H) \;=\; K_{th,\text{air}}\,\exp\!\left(-\lambda_{th}\,\frac{C_H}{C_{\text{ref}}}\right), \qquad K_{th}(C_H) \ge K_{th,\min} $$

SOURCE: phenomenological hydrogen-degradation fit. $\lambda_{th}=1.2$, $K_{th,\text{air}}^{\text{seam}}=3.5$ MPa·√m, $K_{th,\min}=1.5$ MPa·√m — all [ASSUMED], require in-situ testing in pressurized H₂.

Code: src/pit_to_crack.py → degraded_threshold.

4.2 El Haddad intrinsic defect size

$$ a_{th} \;=\; \frac{1}{\pi}\left(\frac{K_{th}}{F\,\sigma}\right)^{2}, \qquad F = 1.12 $$ Transition to a propagating crack is declared when either $K_{\text{pit}} \ge K_{th}(C_H)$ (stress-intensity criterion) or $a_{\text{pit}} \ge a_{th}$ (El Haddad short-crack criterion) is first met.

SOURCE: El Haddad short-crack intrinsic defect size [SOURCE]; geometry factor $F=1.12$ for a semi-elliptical surface crack.

Code: src/pit_to_crack.py → el_haddad_threshold_depth, evaluate_pit_state.

5. Hydrogen-Assisted Fatigue Crack Growth — Modified Paris Law

5.1 Crack-growth rate

$$ \frac{da}{dN} \;=\; C_{\text{paris}}\,(\Delta K)^{m}\,\bigl[\,1 + \alpha_H\,(C_H/C_{\text{ref}})^{\beta_H}\,\bigr] $$ with the enhancement factor $f_H = 1 + \alpha_H (C_H/C_{\text{ref}})^{\beta_H}$ capped at $f_{H,\max}$ to prevent non-physical runaway.

SOURCE: modified Paris law with hydrogen enhancement [SOURCE]. $C_{\text{paris}}=3.0\times10^{-12}$, $m=3.0$, $\alpha_H=15.0$, $\beta_H=0.7$, $f_{H,\max}=50$ — Paris pair and enhancement parameters [ASSUMED] (typical ferritic steel; require calibration in H₂).

Code: src/ha_fcg.py → ha_fcg_rate, hydrogen_enhancement_factor.

5.2 Newman-Raju surface-crack SIF (simplified)

$$ K \;=\; F\,\sigma\,\sqrt{\pi a}, \qquad F = 1.12\,\sqrt{\sec\!\left(\frac{\pi a}{2t}\right)} $$
A constant shallow-crack factor $F=1.12$ with a finite-thickness (secant) correction is used [ASSUMED]. The full parametric Newman-Raju solution (dependence on $a/t$, $a/c$, and angular position) is noted in the source as the production-grade replacement.

SOURCE: Newman-Raju semi-elliptical surface crack, simplified [SOURCE]; $a/c=0.5$ [ASSUMED].

Code: src/ha_fcg.py → newman_raju_simplified.

6. Failure Criterion — Hydrogen-Degraded Toughness

$$ K_{\max} \;\ge\; K_{IC}(C_H), \qquad K_{IC}(C_H) = K_{IC,\text{air}}\,\exp\!\left(-\lambda_K\,\frac{C_H}{C_{\text{ref}}}\right), \qquad K_{IC}(C_H) \ge K_{IC,\min} $$ Fracture is declared when the maximum stress intensity reaches the hydrogen-degraded toughness; wall penetration ($a \ge 0.9\,w$) is a second, competing failure mode.

SOURCE: phenomenological toughness-degradation fit. $\lambda_K=0.8$, $K_{IC,\text{air}}^{\text{seam}}=60$ MPa·√m, $K_{IC,\min}=20$ MPa·√m — all [ASSUMED], require in-situ H₂ testing on actual seam material.

Code: src/ha_fcg.py → degraded_toughness, run_life_prediction.
